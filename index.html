Dwarf-Star

A dwarf star is a star of relatively small size and low luminosity. Most main sequence stars are dwarf stars.

Supernova

A supernova is the explosion of a star. It is the largest explosion that takes place in space.

Wormhole

Wormholes are consistent with the general theory of relativity, but whether wormholes actually exist is not known. A wormhole could connect extremely long distances such as a billion light years

Galaxy

A galaxy is a gravitationally bound system of stars, stellar remnants, interstellar gas, dust, and dark matter

Solar Flare

A solar flare is a sudden flash of increased Sun's brightness, usually observed near its surface. Flares are often, but not always, accompanied by a coronal mass ejection.
